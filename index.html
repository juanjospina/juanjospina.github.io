Summary

I’m a researcher and engineer currently working as a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Los Alamos National Laboratory. I received a dual B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering, an M.S. in Electrical Engineering, and a Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Florida State University in 2016, 2018, and 2019 respectively.

My professional experience is focused on the research and development of intelligent control systems for power systems and smart-grid applications, renewable energy integration, and digital real-time simulation. I believe in a sustainable, secure, and energy-efficient society driven by innovation and automation; that is why I find rewarding working in areas related to machine learning, data analysis, reinforcement learning, optimization, real-time simulation, and cyber-security with applications related to the power and energy fields.

Experience Timeline

Postdoctoral Research Associate

Los Alamos National Laboratory

Mar 2021 - Present

  • Working in the Information Systems and Modeling (A-1) Group at Los Alamos National Laboratory.
  • Developed an open-source Julia package called PowerModelsITD.jl designed for modeling and optimizing integrated transmission-distribution (ITD) power networks tailored for researchers and stakeholders.
  • Developed multiple linear, non-linear, approximation, relaxation, and hybrid ITD optimal power flow formulations designed to accelerate the research in the power system optimization field.

Postdoctoral Research Associate

Center for Advanced Power Systems (CAPS)

Jan 2020 - Mar 2021

  • Developed cybersecurity framework, algorithms, and metrics to improve secure operations in distribution systems with high penetration of DERs using C/C++, Python, and PandaPower.
  • Designed real-time co-simulation cyber-physical systems (CPS) testbeds designed to test and evaluate critical communication and energy infrastructures against cyber-threats and communication degradation scenarios using tools such as Opal-RT, EXataCPS, and the Common Open Research Emulator (CORE).
  • Developed theoretical cybersecurity assessments that utilize deep reinforcement learning techniques (such as Deep Q-Networks) and adapted versions of quantitative cyber-related scores (such as the Common Vulnerability Scoring System (CVSS)) in order to assess the vulnerability of electric power systems with high penetration of renewable systems, and identify the optimal attack path an adversary would need to compromise the system.
  • Analyzed and evaluated the feasibility of cyberattacks on critical infrastructures, such as electric power systems, during the COVID-19 pandemic using statistical analyses and methods such as Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD).
  • Developed quantitative cyber-risk assessment metrics and models designed to provide quantitative scores to the cyber and physical status of an operating CPS. These metrics are intended to provide operating observability of critical components in a CPS, which can then be used by both system operators and sophisticated optimization and control mechanisms in the prospect of creating more secure and resilient systems.
